Training

Learn the difference

Gross Profit

Money left after direct costs

Gross Profit $ = Sell price - Labour cost - Material cost

GP % = Gross Profit divided by the sell price.

This is the main number to target.

Margin

Usually the same as GP %

In everyday business language, margin usually means the same thing as Gross Profit %.

This is why people get confused. Many people use the words interchangeably.

Markup

What you add onto cost

Markup % = Gross Profit divided by the cost.

Markup is based on cost. GP % is based on sell price. They are not the same thing.

Why 30% markup does not equal 30% GP

If a job costs $100 and you add a 30% markup, your sell price becomes $130.

Your gross profit is $30, but your GP % is:

$30 ÷ $130 = 23.08%

That is why markup and GP are different.
